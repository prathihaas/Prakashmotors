## First Question: Is Buying Worth It at 5 Acres?

Let's run the numbers before recommending a model.

### Annual Tractor Hire Cost (Without Own Tractor)

For 5 acres mixed farming (paddy + cotton or turmeric) in Nizamabad:

| Operation | No. of Times | Cost/Acre | Total |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|-------|
| Deep ploughing | 1 | ₹800 | ₹4,000 |
| Disc harrowing | 2 | ₹600 | ₹6,000 |
| Puddling (paddy) | 1 | ₹700 | ₹3,500 |
| Rotavation | 1 | ₹700 | ₹3,500 |
| Inter-cultivation (cotton) | 3 | ₹500 | ₹7,500 |
| Bed formation (turmeric) | 1 | ₹800 | ₹4,000 |
| Transport | varies | ₹2,000/yr | ₹2,000 |
| **Annual hire total** | | | **~₹30,500** |

That's approximately ₹30,000–₹35,000 per year in hired tractor costs.

### Cost of Owning a Tractor (NH 3032 TX, 35HP)

| Cost Element | Annual Amount |
|-------------|--------------|
| EMI (₹7 lakh loan, 7 years, 10%) | ₹1,27,000/year |
| Fuel (farm use only, ~100 hrs) | ₹20,000–₹25,000 |
| Annual service | ₹8,000–₹12,000 |
| Insurance | ₹8,000–₹12,000 |
| **Total annual cost** | **~₹1,63,000–₹1,76,000** |

The math is clear: at 5 acres and personal use only, a new tractor doesn't pay back through saved hiring costs alone. You'd be spending ₹1.6 lakhs to save ₹0.3 lakhs in hire costs.

## When Does Buying Make Sense at 5 Acres?

Despite the above, many 5-acre farmers in Nizamabad do buy tractors and find it worthwhile. Here's why:

### 1. Custom Hiring to Neighbors
If you add custom hiring income to the equation, the picture changes:
- Providing tractor services to neighboring farmers can generate ₹60,000–₹1.2 lakhs per year
- At this income level + your own savings of ₹30,000, you're at ₹90,000–₹1.5 lakhs in annual benefit
- This approaches the ownership cost threshold

In densely farmed mandals like Armoor, Bodhan, or Yellareddy, finding enough hiring work is feasible.

### 2. Timeliness Value
The hired tractor doesn't always come when you need it. During peak kharif sowing or harvest, hired tractors are booked 7–10 days in advance. Missing the optimal sowing window by even 5 days can reduce paddy yield by 8–12%.

For some farmers, the ability to do their own operations at the right time — without depending on a hired tractor — justifies ownership even on a smaller farm.

### 3. Multi-Use Operations
If your tractor also serves for transport, well/pump irrigation management, or other uses, effective utilization increases.

### 4. Growing Farm
If you're likely to take additional land on lease or expand in the next 3 years, buying now makes more forward-looking sense.

## Finance Options for Small Farmers

For 5-acre farmers, financing is well-supported:

**Kisan Credit Card (KCC)**: If you have a KCC with sufficient limit, it can cover the down payment
**PM Kisan Tractor Subsidy**: SC/ST and small/marginal farmers are primary beneficiaries — 25–50% subsidy
**Grameena Bank loans**: Lower interest rates for marginal farmers in rural areas

With a 35HP tractor and maximum subsidy (SC/ST: 50%), the effective out-of-pocket could be as low as ₹4–4.5 lakhs — making the financial case significantly better.
