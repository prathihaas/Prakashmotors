## Dichpally's Farming Profile — Why Soil and Crops Determine Your Tractor Choice

Dichpally's soil is a combination of deep alluvial soil near the Godavari river banks and black cotton soil on higher ground. This mix creates two distinct tillage requirements. Alluvial soil near the river is relatively easy to work but requires good water management during wet paddy season. Black cotton areas on higher ground need strong draught force during dry pre-sowing operations for cotton and maize.

Turmeric cultivation adds the most demanding tillage requirement. Turmeric rhizomes develop best in deep, loose, well-drained beds prepared to 45-60cm depth. Achieving this depth consistently requires a tractor with 47-50HP and a hydraulic lift capacity of at least 1,800 kg to run a heavy bed former or ridger effectively.

[UNIQUE INSIGHT] Dichpally turmeric farmers who use tractors below 45HP on bed preparation often see 15-20% lower turmeric yield due to insufficient bed depth and poorly formed ridges, according to feedback gathered from Prakash Motors service customers in the area. The right tractor HP directly impacts crop income here.
