# New Holland Excel 4510 vs 4710 — Which One to Buy? 2026

*న్యూ హాలండ్ Excel 4510 మరియు 4710 — నిజామాబాద్ రైతులకు ఏది సరైనది?*

The New Holland Excel series occupies an important space in the 45–50HP segment. These tractors bridge the gap between the popular 3630 TX variants and the 55HP Excel models, offering a specific combination of features targeted at medium to large farm operations.

If you're comparing the Excel 4510 and Excel 4710, this guide will clarify the differences and help you make the right call.

---

## Excel Series Overview

The New Holland Excel series is engineered with:
- Higher HP engines for demanding operations
- Multi-speed gearbox options
- Advanced hydraulics
- Operator comfort focused design

Both the 4510 and 4710 are part of this family, differing primarily in horsepower and capability.

## Engine Performance in Telangana Conditions

### Excel 4510 (45HP)
The 45HP engine in the 4510 is smooth and delivers good torque at working RPMs. In Nizamabad's typical farming conditions — paddy, cotton, turmeric — it handles all standard implements effectively.

For black cotton soil operations in Banswada or Yellareddy mandals, the 4510 handles disc ploughing and rotavation confidently. For very heavy implements (sub-soilers, heavy disc ploughs) in hardest conditions, you'll occasionally want more.

### Excel 4710 (47HP)
The 2HP difference sounds minimal, but in actual operation — particularly at high load — the 47HP 4710 maintains working speed more confidently. In Telangana's summer deep tillage (April–May) when soil is at maximum hardness, the extra 2HP provides a meaningful cushion.

The 4710 is also slightly more relaxed in operation — running at lower RPM to achieve the same work, which translates to lower fuel consumption under heavy load (counterintuitive, but true).

**Verdict on engine**: Both are excellent. The 4710's margin matters in heavy, demanding conditions.

---

## Hydraulic System — *హైడ్రాలిక్స్ పోలిక*

The 4710's 1700 kg lifting capacity vs 4510's 1600 kg is a 6% advantage. In practice:
- For standard implements (rotavators, disc harrows, cultivators) — both tractors lift comfortably
- For heavier implements or when working on slopes — the 4710 has more reserve capacity
- For turmeric ridge formers and MB ploughs — both adequate

The hydraulic system response quality is similar on both models.

## Fuel Economy Comparison

In similar conditions, the 4710's higher HP slightly increases fuel consumption at light to medium loads. However:

| Load Level | Excel 4510 (L/hr) | Excel 4710 (L/hr) |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Light (transport) | 1.8–2.1 | 1.9–2.2 |
| Medium (cultivation) | 2.3–2.7 | 2.4–2.8 |
| Heavy (deep tillage) | 2.8–3.3 | 2.9–3.3 |
| Maximum load | 3.3–3.8 | 3.2–3.6 |

At maximum load, the 4710 is actually slightly more efficient — the higher HP means the engine is less stressed, running more efficiently.

## Who Should Choose Which Model?

### Choose Excel 4510 if:
- Your farm is 15–25 acres
- You primarily grow paddy, cotton, or turmeric with standard implements
- Budget is a constraint — the price savings are meaningful
- You don't use very heavy implements
- Light to medium soil types (Armoor, Kamareddy, Yellareddy)

### Choose Excel 4710 if:
- Your farm is 20–35 acres
- You use large implements: 7-ft rotavator, heavy disc ploughs, sub-soilers
- You do custom hiring and need versatility across different farm types
- Your soil includes hard black cotton areas (Banswada, Balkonda, Bodhan)
- You want the best resale value in the Excel series

---

## Price and Value Analysis

The Excel 4710 commands a price premium over the 4510. The premium is typically ₹30,000–₹70,000 depending on variant and current offers.

**Is the premium worth it?**
- For standard farm use on lighter soils: The 4510 may be sufficient
- For demanding operations and custom hiring: The 4710 justifies its premium
- For long-term resale value: The 4710 typically holds slightly better value

**Break-even analysis**: If the 4710 helps you complete operations 10–15% faster or avoid one extra pass in heavy soil over 5 seasons, the premium pays back through time savings and fuel savings.
