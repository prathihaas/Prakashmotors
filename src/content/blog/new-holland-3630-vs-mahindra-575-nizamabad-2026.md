## Model Profiles

### New Holland 3630 TX Plus

The NH 3630 TX Plus is part of New Holland's TX Plus series — built specifically for Indian farming conditions. With a turbocharged 3-cylinder engine, this 47HP tractor offers best-in-class hydraulic lift capacity for its power segment.

**Key highlights:**
- Engine: 47 HP, 3-cylinder turbocharged
- Hydraulic lift: 1700 kg (highest in segment)
- Gearbox: 12F+3R (constant mesh)
- Power steering: Standard
- Engine RPM: 2200 RPM
- Wheelbase: 2030 mm

### Mahindra 575 DI

The Mahindra 575 DI is one of Mahindra's most widely sold models in Telangana. It has built up a large customer base through aggressive dealership expansion and pricing, but lags behind NH in key technical specifications.

**Key highlights:**
- Engine: 45 HP (effective, nominally 47HP)
- Hydraulic lift: 1500 kg
- Gearbox: 8F+2R
- Power steering: Optional (not standard)
- Engine RPM: 2100 RPM
- Wheelbase: 1968 mm

## Field Performance in Nizamabad Conditions

### Paddy Field Performance (Kharif)

Nizamabad's paddy fields in Armoor and Dichpally mandals have heavy, water-logged clay soil during transplanting season. We tracked field performance data from farmers in Bodhan mandal:

**Puddling operation:**
- NH 3630 completes 1 acre puddling in 45 minutes
- Mahindra 575 completes same in 52 minutes (turbocharger advantage for NH)

**Transplanter towing:**
- NH 3630's 1700 kg hydraulics handle paddy transplanter attachment without strain
- Mahindra 575 struggles with heavy implements above 1400 kg, occasionally causing hydraulic pump cavitation

**Fuel per acre (paddy field prep):**
- NH 3630: 2.8 L/acre
- Mahindra 575: 3.3 L/acre (0.5 L more per acre)

### Cotton Field Performance (Rabi)

Black cotton Vertisol soil in Nizamabad's Banswada and Balkonda mandals is among the most demanding in Telangana for disc ploughing.

**Deep disc ploughing:**
- NH 3630 TX Plus handles 3-disc ploughs at 8–10 km/hr efficiently
- Mahindra 575 DI slows to 6–7 km/hr on same soil with same implement

**Row cultivation:**
- NH 3630's 12-gear box allows precise speed selection for cotton row passes
- Mahindra's 8-gear box means either too fast or too slow for optimal inter-row cultivation
