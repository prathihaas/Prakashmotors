## What to Check When Buying a Used NH Tractor

### Hours Meter — Your First Checkpoint

A tractor's hours meter is the equivalent of a car's odometer. A healthy NH tractor used in normal Nizamabad farming (paddy, cotton, 400-500 hours/year) should show 2,000-2,500 hours at 5 years. Under 3,000 hours at 5 years indicates the machine was not overworked and likely has good life remaining.

Be cautious of low-hour readings (under 1,500 hours on a 5-year machine) if the physical condition doesn't match — some hours meters can be tampered with. Check condition of tyres, seat, paint, and hydraulic hoses alongside the meter reading.

### Engine Compression Test

Ask the seller to allow a compression test. A healthy NH tractor engine should show consistent compression across all cylinders. Significant variation (more than 10% between cylinders) suggests uneven wear and potential future costs. White smoke on startup (after warming) indicates oil burning — a concern. Black smoke suggests fuel system issues.

### Hydraulic Pressure Test

Hydraulic system condition is critical for implement performance. Test the 3-point linkage under load: raise a heavy implement (or have the seller demonstrate with their heaviest implement) and hold at maximum height. A healthy hydraulic system maintains position without sinking. Any gradual drop indicates seal wear or pump wear.

Also check all hydraulic hose connections for weeping or dried oil stains — these indicate past or ongoing leaks.

### Tyre Condition

Rear tyres on a Nizamabad tractor wear at 25-30% per 3 years in typical use. A new set of rear tyres costs ₹35,000-55,000 depending on size. Factor this into your offer if the lugs are worn below 50%. Check for sidewall cracking, which can appear on tractors stored in harsh sun — sidewall damage is not repairable.

### Service History from Authorized Center

This is the single most important document check. Service history from an authorized NH center (Prakash Motors or another official dealer) confirms that genuine parts were used and that scheduled maintenance intervals were followed. Tractors without this record are a risk — you cannot verify what was replaced, what was skipped, or what the actual workload was.

## Red Flags — When to Walk Away

Some issues with used tractors are too costly or uncertain to accept. Walk away if you find:

**Cracked engine block or cylinder head:** This is a terminal fault. Repair costs exceed the tractor's value.

**Active hydraulic leaks from the main cylinder:** Indicates internal seal failure. Repair cost ₹15,000-25,000, but may be a sign of systematic neglect.

**Missing or refused service records:** If the seller cannot show service history, you have no basis for assessing maintenance quality. Walk away or discount aggressively.

**Previous accident or frame damage:** Check the front axle, chassis, and transmission housing for weld repairs or misalignment. Even repaired structural damage creates long-term reliability concerns.

**Excessive clutch slip:** Test by loading the tractor on an incline with a heavy implement. If the engine revs but the tractor barely moves, clutch replacement is needed — approximately ₹12,000-18,000 in parts and labor.

## Finance for Used New Holland Tractors in Nizamabad

Banks treat used tractor financing differently from new. Loan-to-value (LTV) for used tractors is typically 60-70% of the assessed value, compared to 85-90% for new tractors. This means you need a larger down payment — expect to arrange 30-40% of the assessed price yourself.

Interest rates for used tractor loans are typically 1-2 percentage points higher than for new: expect 10.5-12.5% annual interest at most banks in Nizamabad. Tenure is typically limited to 5 years (vs 7 years for new). Prakash Motors assists with used tractor financing through HDFC Bank, Mahindra Finance, and select other lenders.

**Important:** The SMAM tractor subsidy does NOT apply to used tractor purchases. Only new tractors purchased from authorized dealers are eligible for SMAM. This is a common misconception among first-time buyers.
