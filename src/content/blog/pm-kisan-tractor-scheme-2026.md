# PM Kisan Tractor Subsidy Scheme 2026 — Eligibility & Application

*PM కిసాన్ ట్రాక్టర్ సబ్సిడీ పథకం 2026 — తెలంగాణ రైతులు ఎలా దరఖాస్తు చేసుకోవాలి?*

Every farmer who wants to buy a new tractor should know about the PM Kisan Tractor Yojana before making the purchase. This central government scheme provides direct financial support to farmers for tractor purchase, significantly reducing the upfront investment required.

In 2026, the scheme continues with important eligibility conditions. This guide explains who qualifies, how much subsidy you can get, and how to apply — specifically for farmers in Nizamabad, Telangana.

---

## What is the PM Kisan Tractor Yojana?

The PM Kisan Tractor Yojana (also called the PM Tractor Scheme) is a central government initiative under which farmers receive a direct subsidy on new tractor purchases. The scheme is implemented through state government agriculture departments and SMAM (Sub-Mission on Agricultural Mechanization).

**Key objective**: Increase farm mechanization by making tractors financially accessible to small and marginal farmers across India.

---

## Subsidy Amount — How Much Can You Get?

The subsidy percentage varies by farmer category:

| Farmer Category | Subsidy (% of tractor cost) | Maximum Subsidy Amount |
|-----------------|---------------------------|----------------------|
| SC/ST Farmers | Up to 50% | ₹3–5 lakhs* |
| Women Farmers | Up to 50% | ₹3–5 lakhs* |
| Small & Marginal Farmers | 25–35% | ₹2–3.5 lakhs* |
| General Category | 20–25% | ₹1.5–2.5 lakhs* |

*Exact amounts depend on tractor price, state government rules, and budget availability. Amounts above are indicative for 2026.

**Important**: These subsidies are subject to budget allocation and application approval. Not all applicants are guaranteed subsidy in the same cycle — apply early.

## Eligibility Criteria

To qualify for the PM Kisan Tractor Subsidy, farmers must meet ALL of the following:

### Mandatory Requirements
1. **Indian citizen farmer**: Must be an active cultivator
2. **Minimum land ownership**: At least 0.5 acres of agricultural land registered in your name (some states have different minimums)
3. **Age**: 18–60 years (some states allow up to 65)
4. **Aadhaar**: Aadhaar card linked to your bank account
5. **Bank account**: Active bank account for direct benefit transfer

### Disqualifying Conditions
The following will make you ineligible:
- You already own a tractor registered under your name
- Your family has already received a tractor subsidy under this scheme
- The same tractor purchase cannot get double subsidy from multiple schemes simultaneously
- Commercial/custom hiring enterprises (as primary business) — different scheme applies

### Telangana-Specific Conditions
For Telangana farmers:
- Land records must be updated in **Dharani portal**
- Pattadar Passbook must be valid
- Farmer registration in PM Kisan portal preferred but not always mandatory

## Eligibility for Nizamabad Farmers — Common Questions

**Q: I have 3 acres in my name and 5 acres in my wife's name. Can I apply?**
A: Yes, if neither of you currently owns a tractor, you can apply. Each eligible farmer can apply once. A family might be able to get subsidy on two tractors if both members meet individual eligibility criteria.

**Q: I already own a 15-year-old tractor. Can I get subsidy for a new one?**
A: This depends on state interpretation. Some states allow subsidy for replacement purchases if the old tractor is scrapped/deregistered. Check with the district agriculture office in Nizamabad.

**Q: My land is jointly owned with my brothers. Am I eligible?**
A: Joint ownership is generally acceptable if your share is properly documented. Consult the district agriculture office.

**Q: I'm a tenant farmer (sharecropper) — am I eligible?**
A: Typically no, as the scheme requires registered land ownership. Some state variations exist for long-term tenants with formal agreements.

## Application Process for Telangana Farmers

### Step 1: Check Your Eligibility
- Verify your land records on Dharani portal (dharani.telangana.gov.in)
- Confirm you don't currently own a tractor registered in your name

### Step 2: Get a Tractor Quotation
- Visit Prakash Motors in Nizamabad and get a formal quotation/proforma invoice for your chosen New Holland model
- The quotation is required for the application

### Step 3: Apply Online (Preferred Method)
- Visit: **https://pmkisan.gov.in** (for farmer registration)
- For tractor scheme specifically: Apply through **state agriculture portal** or **Mee Seva centers** in Telangana
- Required documents for upload (see below)

### Step 4: Apply Offline (Alternative)
- Visit the District Agriculture Office in Nizamabad
- Submit completed application form with supporting documents
- Application is forwarded to state department for processing

### Step 5: Verification
- District Agriculture Officer or mandal agricultural officer visits to verify farm details
- Document verification by department

### Step 6: Approval and Subsidy Credit
- Upon approval, subsidy amount is transferred:
  - Either directly to your bank account (you then pay full amount to dealer, receive subsidy later)
  - Or in some cases, directly to dealer — reducing your purchase price at point of sale

## SMAM Scheme vs. PM Kisan Tractor Yojana

There are two overlapping schemes to be aware of:

**PM Kisan Tractor Yojana**: Direct subsidy to individual farmers on tractor purchase.

**SMAM (Sub-Mission on Agricultural Mechanization)**: Broader scheme covering tractors, power tillers, combine harvesters, and other equipment. Also includes Custom Hiring Center (CHC) establishment.

**For individual farmers**: PM Kisan Tractor Yojana is the primary scheme.
**For entrepreneurs setting up machinery hiring services**: SMAM's CHC component is more relevant.

In addition to central schemes, the Telangana state government provides:

**Kaleshwaram/Rythu Bandhu linked mechanization support**: Check current status with district agriculture office

**SC Corporation subsidies**: Telangana SC/ST Development Corporation provides additional support for SC/ST farmers

**Women Farmers**: Separate provisions under women empowerment schemes

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Buying before applying**: Some subsidy schemes require application and approval BEFORE purchase. Check current procedure — for most cases, the proforma invoice is submitted before actual purchase.

2. **Wrong bank account**: Subsidy is credited to the account linked to Aadhaar. Ensure this is your active agricultural account.

3. **Outdated land records**: Applications with land records not updated in Dharani will be rejected. Update records before applying.

4. **Applying without comparing tractors**: Subsidy makes a good tractor even more affordable. Don't buy a cheaper/unsuitable tractor just because of lower sticker price.

5. **Missing application windows**: Subsidy budgets are finite. Apply early in the financial year when budget is fresh.
