## Why Balkonda's Black Cotton Soil Demands More Power

Black cotton soil, scientifically classified as vertisol, swells when wet and shrinks and cracks when dry. This behaviour creates two distinct tillage challenges across the year. During dry pre-kharif ploughing, the soil becomes rock-hard and requires significant draught force to break open. During wet paddy season, the same soil becomes extremely sticky, creating suction resistance for the tractor wheels and implements.

A 35HP tractor that performs adequately on Yellareddy's red-sandy loam will struggle in Balkonda's black cotton field during peak tillage operations. The engine overloads, fuel consumption spikes, and component wear accelerates. Selecting 47-50HP for Balkonda conditions is not over-specifying. It is the minimum for sustained, efficient operation.
