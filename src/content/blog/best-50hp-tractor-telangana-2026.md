## Why New Holland 4710 Excel Wins — 5 Decisive Advantages

### 1. Highest Hydraulic Lift in Class: 1700 kg

In Nizamabad's black cotton soil, deep tillage implements are heavy. The 4710 Excel's 1700 kg hydraulic lift outperforms every competitor in this table. This means you can confidently attach a heavy disc plough or MB plough without straining the system. Mahindra's 475 DI maxes out at 1500 kg — that 200 kg difference matters when you're running a loaded cultivator through sticky kharif soil.

### 2. Best Fuel Efficiency: 3.8 L/hr vs 4.4 L/hr (Mahindra)

At ₹92/litre diesel price in Nizamabad (April 2026), that 0.6 L/hr difference adds up fast. In a 500-hour farming year, the NH 4710 saves approximately **300 litres** over the Mahindra 475 DI — a saving of **₹27,600 per year**. Over five years, that's over ₹1.38 lakh in fuel savings alone, which more than bridges the initial price gap.

### 3. Superior Gearbox — 12F+3R vs 8F+2R

The 4710 Excel's 12-forward 3-reverse gearbox gives you precise control over speed and torque for every field operation. Cotton row cultivation demands slow, steady movement. Paddy puddling needs a specific speed range. With 12 gears, you always find the perfect setting. Mahindra and Sonalika's 8-gear boxes leave you making compromises.

### 4. Higher PTO Output: 42 HP

PTO-driven implements like rotavators, reapers, and threshers perform better with higher PTO output. The 4710 Excel delivers 42 HP at the PTO shaft — the highest in its class. This translates to cleaner rotavation in a single pass, saving you time and fuel during the hectic kharif sowing window.
