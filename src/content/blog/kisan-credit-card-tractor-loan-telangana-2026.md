## What Is the Kisan Credit Card and How Does It Work?

The Kisan Credit Card is a revolving credit facility — like a credit card, but specifically for agricultural expenses. Your bank sets a credit limit based on your land holding, crop income, and repayment history. You draw from this limit as needed and repay at harvest time. Interest accrues only on the amount drawn.

The standard KCC covers crop inputs (seeds, fertilizers, pesticides) and allied activities. The enhanced KCC — also called the KCC for Allied Activities or Term Loan KCC — extends the limit to include farm equipment including tractors. This enhanced limit is separate from your working capital limit and carries a longer repayment structure (typically 5-7 years for equipment).

The 7% effective interest rate applies when you repay your KCC dues within the stipulated period. The Government of India provides a 2% interest subvention on KCC crop loans up to ₹3L and an additional 3% prompt repayment incentive — bringing the net rate to 7% for timely borrowers ([Ministry of Finance, GoI](https://finmin.nic.in), 2025). For the equipment portion, the subvention structure may differ — confirm with your branch.

## How Much KCC Limit Can You Get for a Tractor in Telangana?

The credit limit for a KCC-linked tractor loan depends on three primary factors: your total land holding, average annual crop income (based on Rythu Bandhu income or bank-verified crop sale records), and existing KCC utilisation.

Most Telangana banks follow NABARD's model guidelines: the term loan component for farm equipment is typically 3-5 times the annual net crop income for the holding. A farmer with 10 acres of paddy and cotton earning approximately ₹3-4L net annually could access a KCC equipment limit of ₹9-20L. This range is wide because individual bank assessments vary.

In practice, Nizamabad district farmers with holdings of 5-15 acres often access KCC equipment limits of ₹4-8L — sufficient to cover the NH 3230 TX (₹5.80-6.20L ex-showroom) to NH Blue Series Simba (₹6.80-7.20L) range. Larger farms with demonstrated income can access limits covering NH 3630 TX Plus and NH 4710 Excel purchases.

## Limitations of KCC for Tractor Purchase

KCC is a strong option but has limitations you should understand before applying.

**Credit limit may not cover the full tractor cost:** A farmer with 5 acres and limited formal crop income may receive a KCC equipment limit of ₹3-4L — insufficient for an NH 3630 TX Plus on its own. In this case, KCC covers part of the purchase and a top-up from a standard bank loan or personal contribution covers the remainder.

**Timely repayment is essential for the 7% rate:** The 7% effective rate applies only with prompt repayment. Missing the repayment deadline means the interest subvention is not credited — your rate reverts to the standard 9-11%. Budget your harvest sale proceeds carefully to ensure on-time repayment.

**KCC assessment takes time:** Unlike a pre-approved car loan, KCC limits are assessed individually. Allow 2-4 weeks for the full process if you are a new KCC applicant or requesting a significant limit enhancement.

**Equipment KCC is separate from crop KCC:** Increasing your equipment limit does not reduce your crop working capital limit. However, both draw from the same overall credit assessment — very high existing KCC utilisation may constrain the enhancement available for equipment.
