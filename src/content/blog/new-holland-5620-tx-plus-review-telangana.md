## TREM IV — What Does It Mean?

TREM stands for **Tractor and Non-Road Engine Machinery** emission standards. TREM IV is India's current highest emission standard for agricultural tractors, aligned with international Tier 4 standards.

**Benefits of TREM IV**:
- Significantly cleaner emissions (reduced particulate matter and NOx)
- More efficient fuel combustion — better fuel economy for given output
- Modern engine management system with electronic controls
- Longer engine life due to better combustion efficiency
- Future regulatory compliance

**Important for buyers**: All new tractor purchases in 2026 require TREM IV compliance. If you see very old-stock tractors without TREM IV — they're outdated and potentially non-compliant for new registration.

## Engine Performance Analysis

### 4-Cylinder Advantage at 75HP

At 75HP, a 4-cylinder engine is the right configuration:
- Better power balance and smoothness than 3-cylinder at this displacement
- Lower vibration frequency — important for operator comfort over long days
- Better cooling efficiency — 4 cylinders can be designed with better thermal management
- More even PTO output for implement operation

### TREM IV Engine Management

The TREM IV engine uses electronic management for:
- Optimized fuel injection timing
- Better low-RPM torque delivery
- Automatic response to load changes
- Overheating protection protocols

In Telangana's demanding summer conditions, this electronic management provides practical benefits farmers notice as consistent, reliable power.

### Power at Working Conditions

75HP at rated RPM is the spec. Real power matters at working RPM (1600–1800):
- **Light load**: Engine runs economically at lower fuel consumption
- **Medium load**: Smooth, controlled power delivery
- **Maximum load**: Engine pulls through without the struggling sound of overloaded smaller tractors

For Nizamabad's deepest tillage requirements — sub-soiling for sugarcane in Bodhan, deep cotton ploughing in Banswada — the 5620 TX Plus handles it in stride.
