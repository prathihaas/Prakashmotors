## What Is the Ex-Showroom vs On-Road Price Difference in Telangana?

Ex-showroom price is what the manufacturer charges before state taxes and registration. In Telangana, the government levies a 9% road tax on tractors, which is among the standard agricultural vehicle rates in the Deccan region. Registration fees at the Nizamabad RTO run ₹8,000-12,000 for a new tractor. First-year comprehensive insurance adds ₹12,000-35,000 depending on the model's insured declared value.

The total on-road cost works out to approximately 12-15% above ex-showroom price. For a mid-range tractor like the NH 3630 TX Plus at ₹7.50 lakh ex-showroom, expect an on-road figure near ₹8.55-8.65 lakh in Nizamabad.

## What Factors Affect the Final Tractor Price?

### Variant and Specification

Most New Holland models come in multiple variants. The NH 3630 TX Plus, for example, has standard and special edition configurations. The difference between base and top variant can be ₹30,000-60,000 for features like a deluxe seat, additional lights, or upgraded tyre specification.

## When Do Government Subsidies Apply?

### SMAM (Sub-Mission on Agricultural Mechanisation)

The Central Government's SMAM scheme offers 25% subsidy (maximum ₹75,000) for general category farmers and 50% subsidy (maximum ₹1.25 lakh) for SC/ST and women farmers. This subsidy applies at purchase time and directly reduces your down payment requirement.

To qualify, you must register on the State Agriculture Portal before purchase. Prakash Motors' team in Nizamabad guides buyers through this registration and submission process at no extra charge.

### Telangana State Schemes

The Telangana government periodically announces additional farm mechanisation grants through Rythu Bandhu Samithis. These are not available year-round. Farmers registered under Rythu Bandhu and with updated Pattadar Passbooks are eligible first. The 2026 state budget included an allocation for farm equipment subsidies for small and marginal farmers (under 5 acres).
