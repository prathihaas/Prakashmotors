## What Makes Yellareddy's Soil Different from the Rest of Nizamabad District?

Yellareddy mandal's soil is primarily red-sandy loam, with black cotton soil pockets appearing near Bheemgal and the Manjira river corridor. Red-sandy loam soil has lower clay content, which means it is easier to till and requires less draught force than the heavy black cotton soil found in Bodhan, Armoor, or Balkonda.

This soil characteristic is important when choosing tractor HP. A 47-50HP tractor is nearly essential in Balkonda's heavy black cotton soil. In Yellareddy, a well-maintained 35-45HP tractor handles most tillage operations efficiently without the extra fuel cost of a larger engine.

The exception is deep summer ploughing for cotton, where the soil hardens into clods in the dry season. For this operation on holdings above 15 acres, a 45-47HP tractor delivers better productivity.
