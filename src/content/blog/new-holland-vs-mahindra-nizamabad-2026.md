Mahindra is India's best-selling tractor brand — a fact that's hard to argue with. It sells more tractors than anyone else in the country, and it has a strong dealer network, reasonable prices, and solid brand recall among Indian farmers. So why do more and more serious Nizamabad farmers, particularly those with 15+ acres of paddy and cotton, switch to New Holland after their first Mahindra?

The answer lies in long-term performance data, not first impressions.

This is an honest comparison based on real ownership experience from farmers in Nizamabad, Bodhan, Armoor, Balkonda, and surrounding mandals.

## The Starting Point: Mahindra's Genuine Strengths

Before comparing, let's acknowledge what Mahindra does well — because ignoring this would not be honest:

**Mahindra's advantages:**
- Lower initial price (₹1–2 lakh cheaper than equivalent NH models)
- Strong resale market — Mahindra is easily bought and sold in local used tractor markets
- Extremely widespread dealer and service network across all of India
- Familiarity — most tractor operators in Telangana have driven a Mahindra

These are real advantages. For a farmer buying their very first tractor on a tight budget with 5–8 acres, Mahindra is a legitimate choice.

**But** — and this is the critical point — for Nizamabad's serious farmers with 15+ acres in paddy and cotton country, the 5-year ownership numbers tell a different story.

## Where Mahindra Wins

**1. Initial Price**
Mahindra 475 DI at ₹5.90–6.40 lakh vs NH 3630 TX Plus at ₹7.20–7.80 lakh — that's a ₹1.30–1.40 lakh difference. This matters for first-time buyers.

Mahindra has a large and liquid used tractor market. You can sell a Mahindra tractor in almost any district of India within days. New Holland's used market, while healthy in Telangana, is more concentrated in districts with NH dealer presence.

## Where New Holland Wins — And It's Not Close

### 1. Hydraulic Lift — The Game-Changer for Black Cotton Soil

New Holland 3630 TX Plus: **1,800 kg hydraulic lift**
Mahindra 475 DI: **1,500 kg hydraulic lift**

In Nizamabad's black cotton soil, farmers typically run heavy disc ploughs weighing 600–900 kg when fully loaded with soil. The difference between 1,500 and 1,800 kg hydraulic capacity becomes visible within the first week of deep tillage. NH handles it effortlessly; Mahindra struggles and often requires multiple passes.

Multiple Bodhan and Armoor farmers report that their Mahindra required **two passes** for deep black cotton disc ploughing, while the NH 3630 completed the same work in **one pass** — saving 3–4 hours per day on large fields.

### 2. Fuel Efficiency — ₹12,000 to ₹18,000 Saved Per Year

This is the number that changes minds. New Holland's turbocharged FPT engine is significantly more fuel-efficient than Mahindra's naturally aspirated diesel engines in the same HP segment.

**Annual fuel saving calculation:**
- Daily fuel difference: 5–7 litres
- Operating days per season: 150–180 days/year
- Fuel price: ₹90/litre
- **Annual saving: ₹67,500–₹1,13,400 total fuel cost** *(NH consistently lower)*
- **Conservative net saving: ₹12,000–₹18,000/year**

Over 7 years: **₹84,000–₹1,26,000 total fuel savings**

This nearly eliminates the initial price advantage Mahindra had on day one.

### 3. PTO Speed Options — Dual vs Single

New Holland offers **540 AND 1000 RPM PTO** as standard on the 3630 TX Plus. Mahindra 475 DI offers only 540 RPM.

Why this matters: Grain threshers and some high-speed implements require 1000 RPM PTO for proper operation. NH farmers run these implements without adapters or workarounds. Mahindra farmers either can't run them or need aftermarket solutions.

### 4. Engine Performance in Hot Conditions

Telangana's summer kharif season sees temperatures of 38–44°C. New Holland's turbocharged intercooled engine maintains consistent power output even in extreme heat. Multiple Nizamabad farmers report that their Mahindra tractors experienced **power loss and overheating** during peak summer operations — particularly during cotton land preparation in May–June.

### 5. Build Quality and Long-Term Reliability

After 5 years and 2,500+ hours, the average New Holland tractor in Telangana requires 20–30% less in cumulative repairs compared to equivalent Mahindra tractors. This is based on service records from Prakash Motors and farmer testimonials across Nizamabad district.
