## Why Sonalika Is Gaining Popularity

Sonalika is India's third-largest tractor brand by volume and competes hard on price. The DI 750 offers 50 HP at a price that undercuts New Holland by ₹50,000–70,000 on paper. In Punjab and Haryana, where Sonalika has dense service coverage, it makes sense for many farmers. The brand has also invested in modern manufacturing at Hoshiarpur.

For North Indian flat terrain and lighter alluvial soils, Sonalika performs adequately. However, Nizamabad's conditions are fundamentally different.

## Four Reasons Nizamabad Farmers Choose New Holland Over Sonalika

### 1. Hydraulic Lift Capacity Matters in Black Cotton Soil

Nizamabad's vertisol (black cotton soil) found in Bodhan, Banswada, Balkonda and Yellareddy mandals is among the heaviest agricultural soil in India. When wet, it swells and becomes extremely sticky. Heavy implements — MB ploughs, disc harrows, rotavators — need high hydraulic lift to pull cleanly out of this soil.

The NH 3630 TX Plus delivers **1,700 kg hydraulic lift capacity**, while the Sonalika DI 750 manages just **1,500 kg**. That 200 kg difference is critical when your subsoiler is stuck 14 inches deep in clay. Farmers in Kubeer and Sarangapur mandals consistently report that underpowered hydraulics cause implement dragging, missed passes, and uneven field preparation.

## When Sonalika Might Still Make Sense

To be fair to Sonalika, if a farmer already has a trusted independent mechanic familiar with the brand, and operates in lighter sandy loam soil (rare in Nizamabad but found near Bhainsa and parts of Nirmal district), the DI 750 is a functional tractor. It is also appropriate for farmers who strictly want the lowest possible upfront EMI and have very small holdings (under 5 acres).
