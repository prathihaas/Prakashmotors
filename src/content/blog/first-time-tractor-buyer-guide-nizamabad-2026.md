## Step 1 — Assess Your Acreage and Crop Needs

Before you look at a single tractor, answer these questions:

- **How many acres do you cultivate?** (owned + leased)
- **What crops do you grow?** (paddy, cotton, turmeric, chilli, maize, sugarcane)
- **What is your soil type?** (black cotton soil in Nizamabad needs more hydraulic power)
- **Do you plan to do custom hiring?** (adds to annual operating hours and EMI justification)
- **Do you have access to an implement shed or parking?**

Your answers determine every subsequent decision. A 5-acre paddy farmer in Armoor has completely different requirements from a 25-acre cotton farmer in Banswada.

A 55HP tractor on a 10-acre farm means:

Match the machine to the mission.

## Step 3 — Evaluate Key Features (Not Just HP)

Once you have your HP range, evaluate these features across brands:

### Hydraulic Lift Capacity
This determines which implements you can attach. For Nizamabad black cotton soil, you need:
- Minimum 1400 kg for 35HP tractors
- Minimum 1600 kg for 47–50HP tractors

New Holland consistently leads on hydraulic lift. The NH 3630 TX Plus offers 1700 kg at 47HP — higher than most 50HP competitors.

### Power Steering Availability
Power steering is no longer a luxury — it's a necessity for daily farming. Tight headland turns in paddy fields and narrow inter-row passes in cotton require minimal physical effort. All NH models from the 3630 TX Plus upward include power steering as standard.

### 4WD Option
If your farm includes uneven terrain, water-logged paddy areas, or heavy towing requirements, select a model with optional 4WD. NH offers 4WD variants on 3630 TX Plus and above.

### Gearbox: Forward Speeds
More forward speeds = more operational flexibility. NH tractors start at 8F gears on the 3230 TX and go up to 12F on the 4710 Excel. This matters for precision operations like transplanting and row cultivation.

## Common Mistakes First-Time Buyers Make

### Mistake 1: Buying on Price Alone
The cheapest tractor has the highest total cost. Fuel inefficiency, frequent repairs, and poor resale value erode your savings within two years.

### Mistake 2: Ignoring the Service Network
A brand with no authorised dealer in Nizamabad means every spare part is a 4-hour wait. In a 10-day sowing window, that can mean missing the entire season.

### Mistake 3: Choosing Too Much HP "to be safe"
Overpowered tractors waste fuel on small plots. A 55HP tractor doing 10 acres of paddy in Armoor is burning ₹15,000 more fuel per year than necessary.

### Mistake 4: Not Claiming Subsidies
Many eligible farmers — especially SC/ST and women farmers — miss subsidies worth ₹1–2 lakh due to lack of documentation help. Prakash Motors assists with all subsidy paperwork at no charge.

### Mistake 5: Skipping the Test Drive
Every tractor brand feels different. Test the clutch, hydraulic lever, seat comfort, and noise level before committing. Prakash Motors offers test drives Monday through Saturday.
