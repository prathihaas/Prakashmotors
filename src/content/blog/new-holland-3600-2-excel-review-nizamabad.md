## Engine Performance — *ఇంజన్ పనితీరు*

The 3-cylinder turbocharged 55HP engine is the heart of the 3600-2 Excel. The turbocharger makes a significant difference compared to naturally aspirated engines in this displacement range:

**Consistent power in heat**: Telangana's summer operations (April–June) are the most demanding. Air temperature above 40°C reduces engine power in naturally aspirated engines by 5–8%. The turbocharger on the 3600-2 Excel compensates for this, maintaining more consistent power output.

**Torque characteristics**: The engine produces strong torque in the 1400–1800 RPM working range. For Nizamabad's heavy black cotton soil in Bodhan and Banswada areas, this translates to consistent pulling power without the RPM hunting that affects lighter engines in heavy soil.

**Responsiveness**: When load increases suddenly (hitting a harder soil patch, or implement depth increasing on a slope), the engine responds quickly without the laboring and power drop common in lower-HP machines.

**Fuel consumption**:
- Light operations: 3.0–3.5 L/hour
- Field cultivation: 3.5–4.5 L/hour
- Deep tillage: 4.5–5.5 L/hour

The 60-litre fuel tank provides 12–15 hours of operation on a full tank for light to medium work.

## Hydraulics in Action — *హైడ్రాలిక్స్*

The 1800 kg lifting capacity with 45 L/min flow represents a significant upgrade from the 1500 kg systems in 47HP models.

In practical terms in Nizamabad's farming:

**Large rotavator operations**: The 3600-2 Excel handles 7-ft rotavators confidently — a capability the NH 3630 TX Plus (47HP) pushes to its limits. In Banswada area's hard soil, where the rotavator encounters varying resistance, the additional hydraulic flow maintains implement response.

**Sub-soiler compatibility**: 3-tyne sub-soilers for deep turmeric preparation in Yellareddy and Armoor areas work well with the 3600-2 Excel's hydraulics. This operation is at the limit of 47HP hydraulics.

**Multi-implement versatility**: The 3600-2 Excel can run a wider range of heavy implements without compromise — important for farmers who switch implements frequently across different crops.

## Maintenance Requirements

55HP turbocharged 3-cylinder:
- Engine oil: Every 250 hours (SAE 15W40 or as specified)
- Turbocharger: Important — always allow engine to idle 2–3 minutes before shutdown; never rev and immediately switch off
- Air filter: Critical for turbo engines — clean every 50 hours in dusty conditions; replace every 500 hours
- Fuel filter: Every 500 hours
- Hydraulic oil: Check monthly; change every 1000 hours

**Turbocharger care in Nizamabad's dusty conditions**: The dry April–May period with sand and dust blowing from harvested fields makes air filter maintenance especially critical. A clogged air filter starves the turbo of air and causes premature wear.
