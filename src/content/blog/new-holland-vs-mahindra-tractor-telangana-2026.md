## Engine and Power Performance

**New Holland** tractors are built by CNH Industrial — one of the largest agricultural machinery companies in the world. Their engines are designed for continuous heavy-duty operation, particularly suited for Telangana's demanding wet paddy fields and deep black cotton soil.

The **New Holland 3630 TX Plus** (47 HP) maintains consistent torque even under heavy load — critical when you are ploughing waterlogged paddy fields near Nizamabad during kharif season.

**Mahindra tractors** use their own TAFE-origin engines which are reliable, well-proven, and easy to repair. The **Mahindra 475 DI** (42 HP) is a workhorse that Telangana farmers have trusted for 30+ years.

**For heavy soil and continuous operation:** New Holland wins.
**For simple, easy-to-maintain power:** Mahindra is competitive.

## Hydraulics and Lift Capacity — Critical for Nizamabad Farming

This is where New Holland stands apart. Lift capacity determines how much your tractor can handle — ploughs, rotavators, and implements for paddy transplanting all depend on hydraulic power.

- **New Holland 3630 TX Plus:** 1700 kg lift capacity
- **Mahindra 475 DI:** 1500 kg lift capacity

For Nizamabad farmers growing paddy, the higher lift capacity means you can use heavier implements without straining the hydraulic system. This directly affects how fast you can complete field preparation before the transplanting window closes.

**Winner: New Holland**

## Fuel Efficiency — Every Litre Counts

With diesel at ₹90–95 per litre in Nizamabad, fuel costs are a major operating expense.

In actual field use reported by Nizamabad farmers:
- **New Holland 3630 TX Plus:** 2.8–3.2 litres/hour under load
- **Mahindra 475 DI:** 3.0–3.5 litres/hour under load

The difference is 0.2–0.3 litres per hour. For a farmer running 10 hours a day during the 45-day paddy season, this adds up to 90–135 litres per season — approximately ₹8,000–12,000 in savings per year.

**Winner: New Holland** on fuel efficiency.

## Build Quality and Durability

New Holland tractors use higher-grade steel in their chassis and implement hitch. Farmers in Nizamabad who have owned both brands consistently report that New Holland withstands the black cotton soil conditions of the region better over a 10-year ownership period.

Mahindra tractors are well-built but designed to be more affordable to manufacture — which shows in certain components like the PTO shaft, axle quality, and hydraulic pump longevity under continuous use.

**For longevity in Nizamabad's tough soil conditions: New Holland.**

## Our Honest Recommendation for Nizamabad Farmers

**Choose New Holland if:**
- You grow paddy or cotton on 10+ acres and need heavy-duty daily performance
- Fuel efficiency matters to you (running the tractor 200+ hours per season)
- You want a tractor that handles deep black cotton soil without strain
- You plan to keep the tractor for 10–15 years

**Choose Mahindra if:**
- Your budget is tight and you need to minimise the initial purchase price
- You grow mixed crops on smaller acreage (5–10 acres) with lighter implement requirements
- Resale in 4–5 years is likely — Mahindra holds second-hand value well
- You already have relationships with Mahindra service staff in the area

**The bottom line:** For the typical Nizamabad farmer with 15–30 acres of paddy and cotton, **New Holland's performance advantages justify the price premium** — especially when government subsidy schemes can offset ₹1–2 lakh of the purchase price.
